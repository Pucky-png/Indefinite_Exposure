Indefinite Exposure

First Term Project

Johannes Dietze

Indefinite Exposure is an exploration of the traces we leave behind. This project invites you to join an anonymous, ongoing collaboration with strangers, contributing to an audio-visual form of storytelling where the individual may be forgotten, but the contributions of the many remain.

Description

Indefinite Exposure is an installation in which users can interact with lost objects to create sounds. These sounds influence live visuals. After users finish with their interaction the sounds they just played are saved and added to a data bank of all recordings created this way. These recordings play whenever no one is interacting with the installation. Indefinite Exposure is a never ending auditory collage of people leaving traces of themselves behind.

Lost Objects

Lost objects forgotten by people with no larger value to the person finding them but of significant value for the person losing them. They are not only a thing that someone lost, they imply things about the character of the original owner. A USB stick with a DJ set on it, a favorite lighter that no longer works, a single earring that is useless without the other. When people lose these things and we find them, we get a little bit of an idea who the person might be, what they like or how they are. They unintentionally leave something of themselves behind.

Technical Setup

Users touch a metal plate and a lost object, this closes a circuit and sends a signal to the MIDI controller board inside of the pedestal. This input is sent to TouchDesigner and plays a previously in Ableton created audio track. The visuals change and respond to the MIDI input and to the audio playing. When the user is done the visuals change back, what they just played is saved to a data bank of recordings created this way. As long as no one is interacting with the lost objects the recordings are playing in the background.

Result

This project's main idea is leaving something of yourself behind, and being part of a greater collaborative work with strangers. Visitors should be confronted with the fact that they likely won't be recognized by what they created but they created something despite that.

My key aspects in creating this experience were non linear storytelling, anonymous collaboration and the fluid switch between observer and collaborator.

The fluid switch between observer and collaborator works because visitors of this exhibition can step up to the pedestal and create sounds just a touch away. When they are done they can just step back and become viewers to previous recordings or others interacting. The non linear storytelling really comes into focus when no one is interacting and the viewer just quietly sits in the room to listen to what others have created. The anonymous interaction comes from the experience of staying longer in one place and people passing by continuously adding their own sounds. This will truly be seen when Indefinite Exposure is part of an exhibition. Ideally it would be placed in a room where people pass by more than once so a corridor connecting other exhibition spaces.

Future Plans

My direct future plans for this project is working on a sudo-randomness so that recordings that came right after one another will be played with each other. I would also improve the current pedestal prototype in making it larger, adding more objects. I am also interested in adding interaction points where users change the sounds by interacting with specific objects. In this way they could either pitchshift existing sound, change between sound banks or modulate the sounds in different ways. The focus on this addition would be to give viewers more possibilities to create individual sound compositions.
